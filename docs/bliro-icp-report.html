Scoring Methodology

How each company's ICP score is calculated

1. Data Source

175 Closed Won deals and 128 Closed Lost deals from HubSpot Main pipeline, mapped to 151 won companies and 94 lost companies. Company firmographics sourced from Speaker (preferred) with HubSpot as fallback. 2 companies appearing in both won and lost were deduplicated by highest deal value.

2. Bayesian-Adjusted Win Rates

Each company is classified into segments across 4 dimensions (size, industry, revenue, geography). Raw win rate = won / (won + lost). To prevent overfitting on small segments, Bayesian shrinkage is applied:

adjusted_rate = (won + 10 × 0.586) / (total + 10)

0.586 = overall base win rate (prior). 10 = prior strength (pseudo-observations). Small-sample segments are pulled toward the average, preventing extreme rates from n<5 groups.

3. ICP Score (0-100)
score = Σ (normalized_rate × weight)

Normalization: Min-max within each dimension -- best segment scores 1.0, worst scores 0.0. This ensures the full 0-100 range is utilized despite Bayesian compression.

Dimension Weight Note
Headcount 35 <50, 50-200, 200-1K, 1K-5K, 5K+
Industry 35 Automotive, Machinery, Chemical, Electronics, Food/Consumer, Other
Revenue 20 If missing, weight redistributed to Headcount + Industry
Country 10 Germany, DACH other, Rest

Revenue Methodology

Data source: HubSpot deal properties -- hs_mrr, hs_arr, hs_acv, hs_tcv, duration_in_months, rollout_potential__in_eur_arr_ -- from 175 Closed Won deals mapped to 151 companies.

Aggregation: Per company, MRR/ARR = max across deals (current run rate), TCV = sum (total contracted), rollout = max (expansion potential).

Expected ARR = adjusted_win_rate x avg_ARR_in_segment. This represents the revenue-weighted probability of winning a deal in each segment -- combining "how often we win" with "how much we win."

Best ARR targets: Segments with the highest expected ARR should be prioritized for outbound. A segment with moderate win rate but high ARR (e.g., 5K+ headcount) may be more valuable than one with high win rate but low ARR (e.g., <50 headcount).
